GATT SETS UP DISPUTE PANEL ON CANADIAN HERRING
The ruling GATT Council set up a formaldispute panel to examine a U.S. Complaint that a Canadian lawprohibiting export of unprocessed herring and salmon wasdiscriminatory.    David Wood, official spokesman of the General Agreement onTariffs and Trade (GATT), told a news briefing the decision wastaken after bilateral consultations failed to resolve the row.    U.S. Ambassador Michael Samuels charged during the Councildebate that Canada was trying to preserve domestic jobs byinsisting that herring and salmon be processed at home.
    Robert White, Canada's deputy chief of delegation, repliedthe law was in line with GATT rules, and was for conservationsreasons. But he agreed to setting up the dispute panel. Reuter
