SOUTH KOREA TO HOLD CURRENT ACCOUNT SURPLUS DOWN
South Korea plans to take steps to keepits 1987 current account surplus below five billion dlrs,Economic Planning Board Minister Kim Mahn-je said.    Kim told reporters the government would repay loans aheadof schedule and encourage firms to increase imports andinvestment abroad to prevent the current account surplus fromrising too quickly.    Last year South Korea's current account surplus was 4.
65billion dlrs. It widened to 622 mln dlrs in January from 484mln in December and compared with a deficit of 334 mln inJanuary 1986, Bank of Korea figures show. REUTER
