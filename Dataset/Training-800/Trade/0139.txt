TAIWAN'S EXPORT ORDERS FALL IN FEBRUARY
Export orders for Taiwanese productsfell 1.37 pct to 3.58 billion U.S. Dlrs in February from 3.
63billion in January, but rose nearly 43 pct from 2.51 billion ayear earlier, an Economic Ministry official said.    He attributed the fall to the rising Taiwan dollar.
    February orders for electric and electronic goods were 619mln U.S. Dlrs, up from 574 mln in January and 370 mln inFebruary 1986. Garment orders were 324 mln dlrs against 383 mlnand 283 mln while footwear orders were 297 mln compared with333 mln and 200 mln. REUTER
