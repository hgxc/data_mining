INDIA GETS 140 MLN DLR WORLD BANK LOAN
The World Bank said it has approveda 140 mln dlr loan for India to help lessen that country'sdependence on imported oil and spur development of its ownpetroleum resources.    The bank said the loan will be used to boost production byinjecting gas in the partially depleted Assam oil fields and toassist exploration in other areas, including drilling 10exploratory wells.    The bank said the recipient of the 20-year loan will be OilIndia Ltd (OIL), which is the smaller of two public Indianpetroleum exploration and production companies.
