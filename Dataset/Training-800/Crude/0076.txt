BRAZILIAN SEAFARERS' STRIKE DAMAGES OIL EXPORTS
A strike by Brazil's 40,000 seafarerswho want pay rises of up to 180 pct may have cost thestate-owned oil company Petrobras 20 mln dlrs in lost exportorders, the company's commercial director Arthur de Carvalhowas quoted as saying in press reports.    More than 170 ships in Brazil, and about nine more inforeign ports, have been halted by the strike, which began onFebruary 27.    Marines began blockading the ships on Friday after thestrike was ruled illegal, and some strikers are running shortof food, National Merchants Marine Union president Edson Areiassaid.
