NYMEX RULE CHANGE SEEN BOOSTING ENERGY TRADE
The New York Mercantile Exchange saidit will introduce exchanges for physicals (EFPS) to its energyfutures markets April one.    An exchange spokeswoman said the change will allow oiltraders that do not hold a futures position to initiate, afterthe exchange closes, a transaction that can subsequently behedged in the futures market.    EFPs, already in effect for platinum futures on NYMEX, areexpected to increase the open interest and liquidity in U.S.energy futures, according to traders and analysts.
    The Commodity Futures Trading Commission approved the rulechange in February, according to a CFTC spokeswoman. Reuter
