CANADA UPHOLDS COUNTERVAIL DUTY ON U.S. CORN
The Canadian Import Tribunal ruled todaysubsidized U.S. corn imports were injurious to Canadian growersand upheld a countervailing duty of 84.9 U.S. cts a bushel setearlier this year.    The ruling is the result of trade action launched in 1985by the Ontario Corn Producers Association that contended U.S.subsidies were driving American corn prices below Canadianproduction costs.
    A spokesman for the Department of Revenue said a study ofthe level of the duty will be undertaken in the next six monthsto determine if it should be adjusted. Reuter
