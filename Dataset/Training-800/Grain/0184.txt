SOUTH AFRICA CROP WEATHER SUMMARY -- USDA/NOAA
Dry weather pushed further intoSouth Africa's Orange Free State's Maize Triangle in the weekended March 14, the Joint Agricultural Weather Facility of theU.S. Agriculture and Commerce Departments said.    In a summary of its Weather and Crop Bulletin, the agencysaid scattered showers continued throughout Transvaal, but drypockets persisted in the northeast and south.    Temperatures average one to four degrees C above normalthroughout all grain areas, stressing grain-filling corn inareas receiving lightest rainfall, it said.
    The agency said rainfall during February was near to abovenormal in most areas, but earlier periods of hot, dry weatherreduced yield prospects in parts of the northern Transvaal andsouthern Orange Free State. Reuter
